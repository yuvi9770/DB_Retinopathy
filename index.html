    Diabetic Retinopathy prediction using Fast-RCNN Classification

    Abstract

    Diabetic retinopathy (DR) is a leading cause of blindness among working-age adults. Early detection and treatment are crucial to prevent vision loss. With the advancements in image processing and machine learning techniques, automated systems for the detection of diabetic retinopathy have gained significant attention. This review provides an overview of recent developments in DR detection methods, focusing on image processing, feature extraction, and classification algorithms. Various imaging modalities such as fundus photography and optical coherence tomography are discussed along with their advantages and limitations. Additionally, deep learning approaches, including convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and their variants, are explored for their effectiveness in DR detection. The review also addresses challenges such as dataset imbalance, interpretability of deep learning models, and generalization to diverse populations. Lastly, future directions and potential research avenues for improving the accuracy and reliability of automated DR detection systems are discussed. Overall, this review serves as a comprehensive guide for researchers and practitioners in the field of diabetic retinopathy detection, aiming to facilitate the development of robust and efficient screening tools for early diagnosis and management of this sight-threatening complication of diabetes.
